Wildfire Information

Wildfires are unplanned fires that burn in natural areas like forests, grasslands, and shrublands. They can spread quickly, destroying homes, wildlife habitats, and natural resources. Most wildfires are caused by human activity, although lightning and extreme weather also play a role.

Alert Definitions

  • Fire Warning: Issued by the Emergency Alert Service in the event of major, uncontrolled fires threatening populated areas and/or major roadways.
  • Fire Weather Watch: Issued by the National Weather Service anytime the area has been dry for a substantial amount of time, the National Fire Danger Rating Systems (NFDRS) is high to extreme, and critical weather conditions are expected within the next 48 hours.
  • Red Flag Warning: Issued by the National Weather Service when there is an ongoing wildfire, or critical weather conditions will occur in the next 24 hours.

VIIRS Fire Observations

VIIRS modeled fire perimeters, derived from the Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS) instrument, provide near-real-time data on active fire locations and intensities, including fire perimeters and active fire fronts, aiding in fire management and wildfire alert systems. This data is sourced from VIIRS, a suite of sensors on board the Suomi-NPP and NOAA20 satellites that collect data on thermal anomalies, including active fires, across the globe. The raw observation data is then used by the Fire Event Data Suite (FEDS) algorithm to generate estimated fire perimeters and fire mean Fire Radiative Power (FRP). The VIIRS sensors overpass at approx. 1:30 AM and PM local time, providing updated estimates of fire evolution approx. every 12 hours. Observations from the most recent overpass are displayed on the map in red.

Causes of Wildfires

  • Unattended campfires
  • Discarded cigarettes
  • Burning debris
  • Arson
  • Lightning strikes
  • Power line sparks

A Short History of Major Wildfires

  • 1871 – Peshtigo Fire (Wisconsin, USA): The deadliest wildfire in American history, killing over 1,500 people.
  • 1988 – Yellowstone Fires: A series of lightning-sparked fires burned over 790,000 acres in Yellowstone National Park.
  • 2018 – Camp Fire (California, USA): The most destructive wildfire in California history, destroying the town of Paradise and killing 85 people.

How You Can Help Prevent Wildfires

  • Follow local burn bans
  • Completely extinguish campfires before leaving
  • Keep fire pits and grills away from dry vegetation
  • Report suspicious activity or unattended fires
